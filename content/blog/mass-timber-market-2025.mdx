## A Market in Motion

The mass timber industry has entered a new phase of maturity. What was once a niche building technology is now a mainstream construction option, with projects spanning residential, commercial, institutional, and industrial sectors across North America.

For suppliers and manufacturers, this growth brings both opportunity and complexity. The competitive landscape is tightening, specification decisions are happening earlier in the design process, and the companies that win are the ones who show up first with the right intelligence.

## Key Trends Shaping 2025

### 1. Earlier Project Engagement Is Critical

Architects and developers are specifying mass timber earlier in the design cycle. By the time a project reaches the bidding stage, the structural system has often already been decided. Suppliers who engage at the concept or schematic design phase have a significant advantage.

### 2. Regional Markets Are Diversifying

While the Pacific Northwest and British Columbia remain strongholds, mass timber projects are accelerating across the Southeast, Midwest, and Eastern Canada. Each region has unique code environments, supply chain dynamics, and competitive players.

### 3. Hybrid Structures Are the Norm

Pure mass timber buildings remain a small segment. The majority of projects use mass timber in combination with concrete, steel, or light-frame wood. Understanding where mass timber adds the most value in a hybrid system is key to effective sales positioning.

### 4. Sustainability Is Table Stakes

Carbon reduction and ESG commitments are driving specification decisions at the institutional level. Mass timber's embodied carbon advantage is well-documented, but suppliers need to quantify and communicate this effectively for each project.

## What This Means for Your Sales Strategy

The days of waiting for RFPs are over. Success in 2025's mass timber market requires:

- **Proactive project identification** — finding opportunities at the approval stage, not the bid stage
- **Relationship-driven sales** — building trusted advisor status with architects, engineers, and developers
- **Data-backed positioning** — quantifying your value proposition with project-specific analysis
- **Regional expertise** — understanding local codes, competition, and market dynamics
